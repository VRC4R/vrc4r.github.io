Who are you?!

Hi! You can call me VRCAR.

I have a sort of facsination with older versions of software, VRChat included. A little over a year ago (April 2021) I indulged in this fascination, looking through archives of VRChat's website and Graham Gaylor's blog just for fun. During my "studies", I found that a good chunk of pre-Steam VRChat builds were still being hosted online, on their original hosts! This got me excited, but I was too busy to channel that excitement and put it to good use, so I recorded my findings in a text file and stowed it away for a while.

A few months ago, I rediscovered that text file, and that spark of excitement reappeared with a new goal: to archive as many VRChat clients and SDKs as I could find. I looked through VRChat's site archives to find existing links to clients, used their filenames to find other clients, discovered the existence of beta/"unstable" clients & tried to find as many of those as I could, too. (I had to work with open directories that had closed directory LISTINGS.) After hours of looking through archives and guessing filenames, I ended up with a pretty extensive list of clients, and archived them all via the Wayback Machine.

That brings us to today, May 22nd, 2022. At the time of writing, I am uploading all the clients I've found onto the Internet Archive, version by version. I don't quite know what this site is going to turn into yet, but for now, it'll serve as this small "About" page you see here. I plan to share the rest of my findings in one form or another in the future, whether it be on this website or otherwise.

If you have any questions, would like to share any findings of your own, or simply would like to get in contact with me, you can reach me at vrcar@riseup.net. Ciao!
